## 1) Core Formula Sheet

## 1.1 Photon Relations

- `E = hf = hc/lambda`
- `c = f lambda`
- Constants often used:
  - `h = 6.63 x 10^-34 J s`
  - `c = 3.0 x 10^8 m/s`
  - `1 eV = 1.6 x 10^-19 J`

## 1.2 Photoelectric Effect

- Einstein equation: `hf = phi + K_max`
- Stopping potential: `K_max = eV_s`
- Threshold relation: `phi = hf_0`

## 1.3 Photon Rate and Current

- Photon power relation: `P = (N/t)E_photon`
- Photoelectric current: `i = ne/t`

## 2) High-Frequency Question Types

## 2.1 Photon Energy and Wavelength Conversion

Typical:

- Given `E` in eV, find `lambda` in nm
- Given wavelength change, compare photon energies

Frequent mistakes:

- Forgetting eV to J conversion
- Wrong powers of ten in nm/m conversion

---

## 2.2 Photoelectric Threshold Logic

Typical:

- Determine whether emission occurs (`f` vs `f_0`)
- Compute work function `phi` from one data point
- Find stopping potential after changing incident light

Frequent mistakes:

- Assuming any light intensity causes emission
- Ignoring threshold frequency condition

---

## 2.3 Graph Interpretation (`K_max-f`, `V_s-f`)

Typical:

- Extract slope/intercept meaning
- Compare different metals from threshold shift
- Predict effect of intensity change on graph

Key facts:

- In `K_max` vs `f`: slope is `h`
- In `V_s` vs `f`: slope is `h/e`
- Changing light intensity does not change slope or threshold

Frequent mistakes:

- Thinking higher intensity increases `K_max`
- Thinking metal change alters Planck constant slope

---

## 2.4 Intensity vs Frequency Effects

At fixed frequency above threshold:

- Higher intensity -> more photons per second -> larger photocurrent
- Maximum electron kinetic energy unchanged

At fixed intensity with higher frequency:

- Photon energy increases
- Photon number per second usually decreases (for same power)
- `K_max` increases if emission still occurs

Frequent mistakes:

- Mixing up current change and energy-per-electron change

---

## 3) Concept Quick Map

- Light behaves as photons in photoelectric effect.
- Emission is immediate (no heating delay model).
- Emission condition is energy-per-photon, not total beam power alone.
- Work function depends on metal type.
- Stopping potential measures maximum kinetic energy.

---

## 4) Fast Solving Templates

## Template A: Find Work Function and Stopping Potential

1. Use one condition with `hf = phi + K_max` to get `phi`.
2. For new light, compute new `hf`.
3. Get `K_max = hf - phi` (if positive).
4. Convert to `V_s` via `K_max = eV_s`.

## Template B: Emission or No Emission

1. Compute photon energy `hf` (or compare `f` with `f_0`).
2. If `hf < phi`, no emission.
3. If `hf >= phi`, emission occurs and `K_max = hf - phi`.

## Template C: Intensity Change Questions

1. Keep frequency fixed.
2. Conclude `K_max` unchanged.
3. Current scales with emitted electron rate.

## Template D: Graph Questions

1. Identify graph type (`K_max-f` or `V_s-f`).
2. Read slope meaning (`h` or `h/e`).
3. Read x-intercept as threshold frequency.
4. Compare metals by intercept shift, not slope shift.

---

## 5) Exam-Day Reminders

- Always check threshold condition first.
- Separate "number of electrons" from "energy of electrons".
- Frequency controls max kinetic energy.
- Intensity mainly controls current.
- Keep unit conversion strict: eV, J, nm, m, V.
